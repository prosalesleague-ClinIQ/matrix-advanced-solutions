The Problem

Weight Loss Has
a Hidden Cost.

GLP-1 receptor agonists have transformed metabolic medicine. Millions of patients are achieving significant, sustained weight reduction. But aggressive weight loss introduces a physiological challenge that is consistently overlooked in clinical practice.

Weight loss does not occur exclusively from fat. During GLP-based therapy or caloric restriction, the body's catabolic response breaks down both fat tissue and lean muscle. Research and clinical observation suggest 20–50% of weight lost may come from lean muscle — not fat.

→
Reduced metabolic rate — Lean muscle is metabolically active. Its loss lowers baseline caloric expenditure and accelerates rebound weight gain.
→
Loss of strength & function — Muscle mass is essential for mobility, stability, and quality of life at every age.
→
Worsening insulin resistance — Skeletal muscle is a primary site of glucose uptake. Reduced mass correlates directly with metabolic dysfunction.
→
Accelerated metabolic aging — Progressive muscle loss drives sarcopenia and age-related metabolic decline.

mTOR Protein Synthesis

Leucine and Beta-Ecdysterone converge on mTORC1 from two distinct upstream pathways. mTOR phosphorylates S6K and releases 4EBP1 inhibition, activating ribosomes to translate mRNA into structural muscle proteins.

MuRF1 Enzyme Inhibition

Maslinic acid directly suppresses the MuRF1 and Atrogin-1 ubiquitin ligases. These enzymes attach ubiquitin tags to muscle proteins, marking them for proteasomal degradation. Inhibition prevents this signal from being executed.

The GLP-1 Challenge

Rapid Weight Loss Accelerates Catabolism

Aggressive caloric restriction upregulates the ubiquitin-proteasome degradation pathway. MuRF1 and Atrogin-1 enzyme levels increase, directly dismantling muscle protein structure for use as an alternative energy source.

mTOR Biology

Protein Synthesis Requires Constant Signaling

Muscle protein synthesis is not passive. It requires continuous activation of the mTOR complex, adequate amino acid availability, and sustained ATP energy — all three of which are compromised during weight loss without targeted supplementation.

Multi-Pathway Design

Single-Mechanism Products Fail at Scale

Muscle preservation during significant weight loss cannot be achieved by targeting one pathway. MuscleLock simultaneously activates anabolic signaling, inhibits catabolic enzymes, and supports the energy infrastructure required to sustain both systems.
